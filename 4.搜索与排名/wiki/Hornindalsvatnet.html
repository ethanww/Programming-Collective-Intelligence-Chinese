Hornindalsvatnet

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search
Map of Hornindalsvatnet.

Hornindalsvatnet is Norway's and Europe's deepest lake, at 514 meters. Its surface is 53 meters above sea level, which means that its bottom is 461 meters below sea level. During the 1990s Telenor, the former national telephone company in Norway, measured the lake's depth at 612 metres while laying a fiber-optic cable along the bottom. Its volume is estimated at 12 km3. Its area is 50 km2 and ranks 19th in area among Norway's lakes. It is located in the county of Sogn og Fjordane and borders on the municipalities of Eid and Hornindal.

The lake is also the site of the Hornindalsvatnet Marathon held annually in July. [1]
