The I-40 Bridge Disaster occurred on May 26, 2002, when a barge collided with a bridge support near Webbers Falls, Oklahoma, causing a 580 foot (180 m) section of the I-40 bridge to plunge into the Arkansas River. The apparent cause was when Joe Dedmon, Captain of the tugboat Robert Y. Love, experienced a blackout. Due to the accident, automobiles and tractor trailers fell into the water, killing fourteen people.

Bridge reopens

An estimated 20,000 vehicles per day were rerouted for about two months while crews rebuilt the bridge. Traffic resumed Monday, July 29, 2002. The reopening set a new national record for such a project, which would usually take six months, according to Federal Highway Administrator Mary Peters.
