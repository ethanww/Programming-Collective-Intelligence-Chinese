Esterel massif

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search
Cap l'Esterel

The Esterel Massif (French: Massif de l'Esterel) is a low mountain range situated in Provence, in south-east France. It borders the Mediterranean Sea between Saint-Raphaël (Var) and Mandelieu (Alpes-Maritimes), though the bulk of the massif is in the Var département.

The range is volcanic in origin and is composed mainly of porphyry, giving the hills a reddish colour. The terrain is rugged, with deep ravines. The highest point of the massif is Mont Vinaigre (618 metres).

The massif covers an area of 320 km², of which 130 km² are officially protected.
