Broadway (microprocessor)

Broadway is the CPU in Nintendo's Wii video game console. It was designed by IBM and will be produced using a 90nm SOI process which will allow the chip to draw 20% less power compared to the Gekko.[1] The Broadway is being produced by IBM at their 300mm semiconductor development and manufacturing facility in East Fishkill, N.Y.[1]

Very few confirmed details have surfaced on this processor. Ars Technica claims that IBM had previously offered the Broadway as a potential laptop PPC 970 processor.[2] Other reports on the Broadway point to a lower power, incremental update to the Gamecube's Gekko CPU.[3][4]

[edit]

Specifications

  • Actual CPU clock frequency and other statistics are still unconfirmed.
  • A 90-nanometer process technology[1]
  • Has a Power Architecture core specially modified for the Wii platform.[1]
  • The chips will feature IBM Silicon on Insulator (SOI) technology which will allow the chip to draw 20% less power compared to the Gekko.[1]
  • It was confirmed that the CPU has been manufactured and have been shipping to Nintendo since July.
[edit]

Production

The chips are being produced by IBM at their state-of-the-art 300mm semiconductor development and manufacturing facility in East Fishkill, N.Y.[1]
