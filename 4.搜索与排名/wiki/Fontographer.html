Fontographer is a software application used to design fonts, available for both Microsoft Windows and Apple Macintosh platforms.

Fontographer was originally developed by Altsys for the Apple Macintosh. Released in 1985, before Adobe Illustrator, it was the first commercially available Bézier curve editing software.

On January 1995, Altsys was acquired by Macromedia. Although development of the software was frozen since 1998, when version 4.1 was released, many font designers continued to use it.

On May 2005, FontLab Ltd. announced that they had licensed distribution rights from Macromedia, and resumed development. They are currently selling Fontographer along with their other products. In December 2005 FontLab shipped a new version of Fontographer for Mac OS, running natively on OS X and featuring numerous bug fixes. They have since exercised their option to buy all rights to Fontographer, so it is now fully owned by FontLab Ltd.
