Curtis Priem

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Curtis Priem helped cofound NVIDIA and was its Chief Technical Officer from 1993 to 2003. He had been the architect for the first graphics processor for the PC, the IBM Professional Graphics Adapter. From 1986 to 1993 was senior staff engineer at Sun Microsystems where he developed the GX graphics chips. He is also currently (2005) a trustee of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, his alma mater.

In 2001 RPI received an anonymous gift of $360 million, at the time the largest individual contribution to a university. It has been rumored that Priem was the anonymous donor.[1]
