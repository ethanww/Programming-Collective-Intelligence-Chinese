Sanskrit numerals

The Sanskrit alphabetic numerals were created in about A.D. 510 by Āryabhaṭa. The system is also known as Āryabhaṭa numeration.

History

[edit]

Example

 
299, 792 468
Two hundred ninety-nine million, seven hundred ninety-two thousand, four hundred sixty-eight
equals
जल घिनि झुशु झृसृ खॄ
ja-la,  ghi-ni,  jhu-śu,  jhṛ-sṛ,  khḷ.
 
To understand this example, one have to know that in the traditional Indian notation of numbers and thus also in their denomination, the order of the digits was inversed by comparing to our modern way. So, naturally, also Āryabhaṭa began with the ones, before the tens; than the hundreds and the thousands; than the myriad and the lakh ( = 105 ), a.s.o. Like it shows this more detailled table just below:
 
Example:  299, 792 468
100 101 102 103 104 105 106 107 108
 85,  42,  97,  99, 2
जल घिनि झुशु झृसृ खॄ
ja-la ghi-ni jhu-śu jhṛ-sṛ khḷ
