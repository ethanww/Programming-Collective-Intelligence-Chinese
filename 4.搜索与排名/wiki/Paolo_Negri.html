Paolo “Apollo” Negri is an Italian jazz musician born in 1979. He began his musical career in 1992 with a progressive rock trio called Nice Price, releasing a mini-album and a few compilations. The Hammond organ, his fundamental instrument, is soon reached by several vintage machines such as Moog and ARP synthesizers, electric pianos, Eminent and Mellotron. In 1997 he started his collaboration with The Link Quartet, a sixties lounge/acid jazz ensemble. With this band he publishes two 45 singles and the first album, for the Spanish label Animal Records, two albums for the American Hammondbeat Records (the same label that will publish a series of official bootlegs of the band) and one album for the Italian Recordkicks label.

The Link Quartet is involved in several compilations and works with some of the most important labels from Europe and USA (such as Sony, Acid Jazz, Saifam). With this band Paolo performs all over Europe and, in 2003 and 2004, all over USA too, meeting on the stage musicians like Manu Chao, Brian Auger, The Yardbirds, James Taylor Quartet, Bonniwell Music Machine, The Chocolate Watchband, Big Boss Man, The Men from S.P.E.C.T.R.E.,Diplomats of Solid Sound, Downliners Sect, Montefiori Cocktail, Boogaloo Investigators and many more.

In the year 2000 he joins the Wicked Minds’ line up, a hard-progressive band with whom he realizes three albums, the first for the Italian W-Records label and the following two for the Italian Black Widow Records label, the second one of those followed by a live DVD, receiving the ovation from a great number of specialized magazines all over the world and performing across Europe, sharing the stage with Delirium, Areknames, Osanna, Mr. Man, Colour Haze, Siena Root, Birth Control anmong others and appearing, at the Burg Herzberg Festival in 2006 in Germany with Soft Machine, UFO, Wishbone Ash, Hatfield & The North and Greg Lake. In 2001 he plays on the main stage of Arezzo Wave with John Belpaese, opening for Max Gazzè. In 2002 starts his collaboration with Ray Daytona and Googoobombos and realizes “Fasten seat belt” album for Ammonia Records (with an incredible cover by Winston Smith). The album is followed by an intense live tour in 2003. In the same period he collaborates with Titty Twisters Orchestra recording the album for the Italian Gonna Puke Records label and playing at the Wild Weekend in Spain.

In 2005 he joins Mastica, a psychedelic progressive rock band considered one of the best bands in the sixties circuit. After many gigs all over Europe including performances at the Magic Festival in Spain, the Beat Bespoke in London and a tour in Finland and Sweden, releases a ’45 single on the Swedish label Crusher Records and an LP and a CD for the Italian Misty Lane Records. In 2006 the band is again in studio recording their new full length album for the Italian Sound and Vision, supported by Venus distribution. In 2006 he started a collaboration with DJ Henry Lazzeri, well known in the mod scene, creating Party Hard, an explosive duo devoted to Hammond groove and soulful house, and records his first solo project, “Applecore”, a loving instrumental tribute to ’60 soul-jazz underlining his unique style and skill on the Hammond organ.

Considered to be one of the finest Hammond players in Europe, he also collaborates as a graphic designer with W-Records, Black Widow Records and Hammondbeat under the nickname of “Apollo’s garden of earthly delights”. In the studio he has collaborated with: The Rookies, Je ne t’aime plus, David Stockdale, No Limits, Sim, Anatre Supreme, Kim’s Teddy Bears, Gianluca Pighi, Paisley Windowpaine, John Belpaese, Vendetta among others.
