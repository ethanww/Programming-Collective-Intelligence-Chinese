A VJ or veejay, is a person who introduces and plays videos on a video music channel or show. The term is derived by the seldom used term "video jockey," a derivative of the term "disc jockey", "DJ" (deejay) as used in radio. The term was popularized by the Music Television Network (MTV) in the 1980s. A veejay is someone who introduces music videos on television, much like a radio DJ would play records or CDs. Downtown Julie Brown was a popular Veejay on MTV.

Other terms used are VDJ for "Video DJ" or MVJ for "Mobile VJ."
