History

The ST group was formed in June 1987 from the merger of two semiconductor companies: the Italian company SGS Microelettronica (Societ√† Generale Semiconduttori) and the French company Thomson Semiconducteurs, the semiconductor arm of the French company, Thomson. In May 1998, the company changed its name from SGS-THOMSON to STMicroelectronics following the withdrawal of Thomson SA.

Looking back farther:

  • The Italian company SGS was initially named SGS-ATES (Aquila Tubi E Semiconduttori) and resulted from the merger in 1972 of the company ATES (Aziende Tecnica ed Elettronica del Sud), initially founded in 1963 and the company SGS (Societ√† Generale Semiconduttori), founded in 1957 by Adriano Olivetti.
  • The French company Thomson Semiconducteurs was created in 1982 with the merger of :
    • The semiconductor activities of the French electronic company Thomson.
    • Mostek, a US company founded in 1969 by some of the founders of Texas Instruments.
    • Silec, founded in 1977.
    • Eurotechnique founded in 1979 in Rousset, Bouches-du-Rh√¥ne, France near Aix-en-Provence. It had been created as a joint-venture between the French company Saint-Gobain and the American company National Semiconductor.
    • EFCIS, founded in 1977.
    • SESCOSEM, founded in 1969.

In 1989, SGS-Thomson purchases the British company Inmos, formerly known for its transputer microprocessors. In 1994, SGS-Thomson purchases the semiconductor activities of the Canadian company Nortel.

Share-holders and revenues

After the merger of SGS and Thomson Semiconducteurs, the balance was maintained between French and Italian shareholders. 8 December 1994, the company completed its initial public offering. Since then, shares have been traded on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: STM) and on Euronext, Paris (Euronext: STM). Since June 1998, ST has also been listed in Milan on Borsa Italiana.

In 2005, the balance of the shares is held by STMicroelectronics N.V. Of the shareholders:

  • 72.4% are public.
  • 27.6% are split 50/50 between Italian shareholders (40/60 between the Italian company Finmeccanica and the Cassa Depositi e Prestiti) and French shareholders (the French company Areva).

The holding STMicroelectronics N.V. is registered in Amsterdam, Nederland. But the corporate headquarters, as well as the headquarters for Europe and for Emerging Markets, are in Geneva. Competitors

When created in 1987, SGS-Thomson pointed at the 14th position of the TOP 20 semiconductors suppliers ranking with around $850 Millions USD. In 2005, 'ST's position was number five , behind Intel, Samsung, Texas Instruments and Toshiba, but ahead of Infineon,Renesas, NEC, Philips, and Freescale. ST is also the first European semiconductors supplier, just ahead of Infineon and far ahead of Philips.

Product portfolio

The company holds a large portfolio. ST is leader in many markets including :

  • Digital consumer application specific standard products (ASSPs) like coders/decoders MPEG-2/MPEG-4 .
  • Wireless ASICs.
  • Computer peripheral ASIC (like hard disk drives or printers).
  • Automotive application specific standard products (ASSPs).
  • Non-volatile memory including NOR and NAND Flash memory
  • Smartcards
  • Analog and power integrated circuits (ICs)
  • Microcontrollers
  • Discrete semiconductor products

Since 2001 ST has been involved in developing MEMS at the Castelletto site. This plant has been closed in June, 2006 and the MEMS activities moved to the Agrate main fab.

Since the beginning, ST took the decision to stay out of the markets for DRAM and microprocessors for PC. A brief exception occurred with the small and limited attempt in 1994 to introduce compatible Intel x486 microprocessors in partnership with the American company Cyrix and the 1995 production of the Cyrix M1 microprocessor, a competitor to the Intel Pentium family.

The company totals around 1500 customers. The most important are :

  • The automotive equipment suppliers  : Bosch, DaimlerChrysler, Visteon, and Siemens
  • The cellular phones suppliers : Nokia, Motorola
  • The printer suppliers : Hewlett-Packard
  • The telecom infrastructure suppliers : Alcatel and Nortel Networks
  • The hard disk drives suppliers : Seagate Technology and Western Digital
  • The consumer electronics suppliers : Philips, Sony, and Thomson
  • The industrial equipments suppliers : Siemens
  • The electronic components distributor Arrow Electronics

Company organization

The company is based on four top organizations :

  • The Product Groups
  • The Sales, divided in five regions ((Europa, USA, Asia/Pacific, Japan, China and Emerging Markets). The group totals 78 sales offices in 36 countries.
  • The manufacturing facilities.
  • The corporate R&D group.
[edit]

Products Groups and R&D

There are five Product Groups, each groups is composed of several divisions. Each division manages itself the design, industrialization, manufacturing (using the ST manufacturing facilities) and marketing for its product portfolio. They are helped by the corporate R&D and the local sales offices.

  • HPC : Home Personal Communication : All consumer, multimedia & wireless/wireline divisions.
  • MPG : Memory Product Group : All stand-alone memories : EEPROM, Flash memories (nand & nor), serial flash memories and also smartcards.
  • APG : Automotive Product Group : All chips related to Automotive business (car body , power train, safety, etc.
  • MPA : Micro, Power & Analog Group : All analog & power circuits plus the microcontrollers.
  • CPG : Computer Peripheral Group : All chips related to Computer Peripherals : (Hard Disk Drive Controllers, Printers, etc)

The corporate R&D labs (now FTM: Front End Technology and Manufacturing) are developing leading-edge process technology and innovative design methodologies. In addition, each division operates its own R&D to design, industrialize, and build the applications of its products.

The group totals 16 advanced research and development units, 39 design and application centers.

[edit]

Manufacturing facilities

ST is a chip maker which owns its wafer fab (on the contrary to fabless companies). In 2006, the company owns five 8" wafer fabs and one 12" wafer fab. Most of the production is done with technology nodes of 0.18¬µm, 0.13¬µm, 90nm and 65nm (corresponds to the length of the gate transistors which composes the electronic chip). ST also owns many back-end plants. It consists of factories where the silicon dies are assembled and bonded into plastic or ceramic packages.

Major sites

Here are the major sites, ordered by importance :

[edit]

Grenoble and Crolles, France

Around 6000 employees.
With Milan, it is the most important plant of the company. Actually, it consists of two separate sites :

  • Grenoble Polygone, in the center of the city, near the station. (2200 employees).

This is one of the historical base of the company (ex THOMSON). All the historical wafer fab lines are now closed. This site is the headquarter of many divisions (marketing, design, industrialization) and consists of an important R&D center, focussed on silicon & software design and fab process development.

  • Crolles, in the suburb of Grenoble, in the direction of Chamb√©ry.

The site hosts a 8" fab and a 12" fab. The site is born with the partnership (named Grenoble 92) signed in 1990 between SGS-Thomson and the CNET (The R&D center of the French telecom company France Telecom also known as Orange) to build a common R&D center for submicron technologies. In addition, Philips and SGS-Thomson signed in 1991 a partnership to develop together the new manufacturing technologies at Crolles. As a result, the first 8" fab of the company (Crolles 1) was inaugurated on 9 September 1993 by G√©rard Longuet, the French minister of the industry. In April 2002, ST, Philips and Freescale signed a partnership to create once more in Crolles a new common R&D center to develop the new nanometric technology process from nodes 90nm to 32nm using 12" wafers. The Taiwanese company TSMC, the first worldwide semiconductor foundry is also involved in the partnership to set-up the technologies in Taiwan and complete the production capacity. This is what is known as The Crolles 2 Alliance. On 27 February 2003, the 12" fab was inaugurated by Jacques Chirac, the French president.

[edit]

Milan and its suburb, Italy

Around 6000 employees.
With Grenoble, it is the most important plant of the company. It consists in fact of two separate sites :

  • Agrate, in direction of Venice (around 4000 employees).

With Grenoble, this is the other historical base of the company (ex SGS). The site counts several fab lines (including an 8" fab) and consists of an important R&D center. Many divisions' headquarters are located in Agrate.

  • Castelletto, in direction of Genova (300 to 400 employees).

This historical site hosts some divisions and R&D centers.

[edit]

Catania (Sicily), Italy

4800 employees.
This plant is born in 1961 with the implantation of the company ATES. The semiconductors activities (initially Germanium) started with some licensing agreements with the American company RCA. Now, this site counts two major wafer fabs of the company ;

  • an 8" fab, inaugurated in April 1997 by Romano Prodi, president of the italien council. This consists of the 3rd 8" fab after Crolles-1 and Phoenix.
  • a 12" fab under construction.

The site also hosts many R&D centers and divisions (especially for flash memory technologies)

[edit]

Rousset, near Aix-en-Provence, France

Around 3000 employees.
The site has been created in 1979 with the building of a 4" fab by the French company Eurotechnique. Eurotechnique was formed by a joint venture between the French company Saint Gobain and the american company National Semiconductor. It was sold to Thomson-CSF in 1982 following the nationalization of several companies decided by the French government in 1981/82. At this time, the old Thomson plant located in the center of the city of Aix-en-Provence (initially created in the sixties) closed and people were transferred to the new Rousset site. In 1988, a few number of people of the Thomson Rousset plant (including the director, Marc Lassus) founded a start-up company, formerly known as Gemplus, now a big company named Gemalto, leader in the smartcard industry. The company started with the important business of the first French telecom chip cards. The historical 4" fab has been successively converted into a 5" and then into a 6" fab (completed in 1996). This fab is now in the process of being closed soon. In 1995, ST announced that it will build its fourth 8" fab in Rousset (after Crolles-1, Phoenix and Catania). The fab was inaugurated on 15 May 2000 by the French prime minister Lionel Jospin. The site hosts several division headquarters (like smartcards, microcontrollers, serial flash, EEPROM) and several R&D centers.

[edit]

Ang Mo Kio, Singapore

2000 employees.
In 1970, SGS created its first assembly back-end plant in Singapore, in the area of Toa Payoh. Then in 1981, SGS decided to build a wafer fab in Singapore. The singapore technical enginneers have been trained in Italy and the fab of Ang Mo Kio started to produce its first wafers in 1984. Converted up to 8" fab, this is now an important 8" wafer fab of the group. Ang Mo Kio also hosts some design centers.

[edit]

Greater Noida, near Delhi and Bangalore, India

1500+100 employees.
The site of Noida is born in 1992 with some software engineering activities. Then, a silicon design center has been inaugurated on 14 February of 1995. With 120 employees, it was the larger design center of the company outside Europe. In 2006, the site counts around 1500 employees and has been transferred some kilometers further at Greater Noida. The site hosts mainly design teams.
The site of Bangalore is much more recent and totals around 100 employees.

[edit]

Phoenix, Arizona, U.S.

In the beginning of the eighties, SGS created its first plant in the USA with the implantation of a sale office in Phoenix. Later on, with SGS-Thomson, this site acquired another dimension with the construction of an 8" fab, completed in 1995. It was the second 8" fab of the company after Crolles 1, firstly dedicated for the production of the microprocessors for Cyrix. It is now an important 8" fab of the manufacturing machine of the company.

Tours, France

1500 employees.
This site hosts a fab and R&D centers.

[edit]

Carrollton, near Dallas, Texas, U.S.

This site has been created in 1969 by the American company Mostek (founded by some old employees of Texas Instruments). Mostek has been purchased firstly by United Technologies before being purchased by Thomson Semiconducteurs in 1985. Initially equipped with a 4" fab, it has been extended to a 6" fab in 1988. The American activities of the British company INMOS of Colorado Springs have been transferred in Carrolton, following its acquisition in 1989. Then, the site focussed on wafer tests.
