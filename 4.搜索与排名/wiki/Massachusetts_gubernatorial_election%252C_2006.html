Massachusetts gubernatorial election, 2006

The Massachusetts gubernatorial election of 2006 is to be held on November 7, 2006 and winners will serve a four-year term, from January 4, 2007 until January 6, 2011. Every four years, Massachusetts holds elections for Governor and Lieutenant Governor, as part of the general statewide election. The Primary election is Tuesday, September 19, 2006.

[edit]

Governor

One-term Republican governor Mitt Romney is not seeking re-election and will leave office when his term ends in January 2007. Polls had been mixed prior to Romney's announcement, with one poll showing Romney slightly leading Democrat Attorney General Tom Reilly and other polls showing Reilly in the lead.[1] Romney has endorsed Lieutenant Governor Kerry Healey to succeed him in the 2006 gubernatorial election. As of March 2006, Republican Christy Mihos has left his party to run as an Independent, and the Green-Rainbow Party have nominated a slate of candidates for statewide office. It is unclear whether the Libertarian Party will be running candidates state-wide in this election, though they may run a candidate for U.S. Senate against Ted Kennedy. State law requires that candidates for state-wide office be registered in their party (or unenrolled for independent candidates) by March 7.

At the Democratic Convention on June 3, each candidate to be on the primary ballot in September needed to receive support from 15% of the delegates, and there was some question as to whether Chris Gabrieli could succeed after entering the race so late. Deval Patrick received the convention's endorsement with 57.98% of the vote, Tom Reilly made it with 26.66%, and Chris Gabrieli just made it with 15.36%.[2][3]

On August 11, it was discovered by Joan Vennochi of the Boston Globe that Tom Reilly's campaign had been coordinating with an attempt to swiftboat Deval Patrick over his experience at Coca-Cola.[4][5] The "Campaign to Stop Killer Coke", a group dedicated to holding Coca-Cola accountable for violence in its Colombian bottling plant in the mid-1990s (see Criticism of Coca-Cola for more), had began to attack Patrick and his candidacy, despite the fact he resigned the company after his attempts to get them to carry out an indpendent invesitigation were ignored and undermined[6]. Before the revelation of the Reilly campaign involvement, five Massachusetts unions had filed a complaint against the group with the Office of Campaign and Political Finance,[7] a move supported by Secretary of State candidate John Bonifaz[8] and based on research by David Kravitz of Blue Mass Group.[9] On Sunday, September 10th, 2006, The Boston Globe endorsed Deval Patrick as governor, a key endorsement, considering that The Globe is newspaper of record and also the most prestigious newspaper in Massachusetts. [1]

Democratic candidates

  • Chris Gabrieli - Businessman, 2002 nominee for Lieutenant Governor. Officially announced candidacy April 6th, 2006. [10]
  • Deval Patrick - Ex-Assistant United States Attorney General for Civil Rights & Ex-Corporate Legal Executive
  • Thomas Reilly - Attorney General & Ex-Middlesex County District Attorney

Green-Rainbow candidate

  • Grace Ross - Long-time Green-Rainbow Party activist and former party Co-Chair, she has been heavily involved in the March to Abolish Poverty campaign and is the first openly gay woman to run for governor.[11]
[edit]

Republican candidate

  • Kerry Healey - incumbent Lieutenant Governor, elected in 2002 on ticket with Mitt Romney; has a background as a criminologist, former Massachusetts Republican Party chairwoman.
[edit]

Unenrolled candidates

  • Christy Mihos - Owner of Christy's Convenience Stores on Cape Cod, and former member of the Massachusetts Turnpike Authority board[12]
[edit]

Write-In candidates

  • John Hawkins - Socialist Workers Party candidate for governor: a meat cutting worker, Black rights activist, and "promoter of the march on Washington demanding no U.S. intervention in the internal politics of Cuba and Venezuela".[13]
[edit]

Lieutenant Governor

As the incumbent Lieutenant Governor is running for Governor, the position is open. In Massachusetts, the Lieutenant Governor candidates are nominated separately from the Gubernatorial candidates. Then the nominated pairs from each party run together as a team.

On April 23, 2006, a "virtual debate" between Democrats Sam Kelley, Tim Murray, and Andrea Silbert was released on SaintKermit.com, hosted by Jim Henderson and 2002 Green-Rainbow Lieutenant Governor candidate Tony Lorenzen. The Greater Lowell Area Democrats, the Lowell Democratic City Committee, and BlogLeft held a debate between all four candidates on May 21 at the Lowell Telecommunications Corporation, was broadcast on public access TV and is available online. Four days later on May 25, Dr Sam Kelley dropped out of the race and joined the Deval Patrick campaign as a volunteer advisor on health care issues.[14]

At the Democratic convention on June 3, Worcester Mayor Tim Murray was endorsed by a voice vote after receiving 49% on the first ballot. Deb Goldberg and Andrea Silbert both qualified for the ballot with 22% and 29% respectively. Christy Mihos announced on June 8 that John Sullivan would join him as his running mate.[15]

[edit]

Democratic candidates

  • Deborah Goldberg - Former chairwoman of the Brookline Board of Selectmen and granddaughter of the founder of Stop & Shop.
  • Tim Murray - Mayor of Worcester
  • Andrea Silbert - Businesswoman, co-founder and former CEO of the Center for Women & Enterprise, a non-profit entrepreneurial training center.
[edit]

Green-Rainbow candidate

  • Martina Robinson – former Belchertown selectman candidate and disability rights activist picked by her running mate, Grace Ross, on the deadline, September 7. She is confined to a wheelchair, as she suffers from cerebral palsy, and has been involved for 15 years with the American Disabled for Attendant Programs Today (ADAPT).[16]
  • Wendy Van Horne – dropped out of the race on September 1.[17]
[edit]

Republican candidate

  • Reed V. Hillman - a former state representative and colonel of the Massachusetts State Police, he was chosen by Kerry Healey to be her running mate in early March 2006.
[edit]

Unenrolled candidate

  • John J. Sullivan - Town moderator of Winchester, former selectman, and chair of the Winchester Co-operative Bank. A former Democrat, he disenrolled in March and is now Christy Mihos's running mate.
[edit]

Former candidates

Four candidates have dropped out of this race:

  • Chris Gabrieli (D) - Businessman, 2002 nominee for Lieutenant Governor. Declined Tom Reilly's offer to be running mate and is now running for Governor (see above).
  • Sam Kelley (D) - Child psychologist and former legislative aide to Congressman Jim McDermott
  • Marie St. Fleur (D) - State representative. Picked by Reilly to be his running mate, but dropped out the next day.[18]
  • Wendy Van Horne (J) - Former Co-chair of the Green-Rainbow Party and a nurse, was Grace Ross's running mate until Sept 1 At 22, she was not only the youngest person to run for Lieutenant Governor in recent history, but also the first Asian-American nominee for statewide office.
