List of Norwegian companies

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

This is a list of Norwegian companies operating as of 2004, and historical companies.

[edit]

Operating

  • 24SevenOffice, web-based ERP/CRM software.
  • Advista AS, IT solutions and development of directories
  • Aker Kværner, offshore industry, shipyards, oil and gas
  • Atmel Norway, electronics (microcontrollers)
  • SAS Braathens, airline (part of Scandinavian since 2001)
  • Chipcon, semiconductors (mostly RF chips)
  • Coast Air, short-haul airline
  • Conduct, technology consultancy
  • Den norske Eterfabrikk, pharmaceuticals, chemicals and cosmetics
  • Dolphin, computer communications (mostly SCI)
  • Ekornes, furniture
  • Elkem, industry
  • Eltek, electrical equipment
  • Ementor, IT solutions and services
  • Fast Search & Transfer, IT
  • Findexa, media and information
  • Figgjo, professional China manufacturer
  • Fjord1 Nordvestlandske, transport, esp. ferries
  • FlaskePost Medieutvikling, graphic design & digital services within foto & video
  • Fred Olsen, shipping and industry
  • Freia, chocolate and sweets
  • Frontline Limited, shipping (oil tankers)
  • Funcom, computer and video games development
  • Ibas, data recovery and computer forensics
  • Innerloop, computer game development (JSF, Project IGI 1&2 etc.)
  • Jotun, paint and powder coating
  • Kollega Bil, electric cars
  • Kongsberg Gruppen, defence and maritime industry
  • Kverneland, agricultural machinery
  • Kvaerner, industry
  • Laerdal, medical equipment
  • Linpro, free and open source systems
  • Mantena, performing research, maintenance, repairs and servicing on rolling-stock
  • Nera, wireless telecom equipment
  • Nettkatalogen AS, directory with all norwegian companies and persons
  • NextGenTel, ADSL, ADSL2+ and SHDSL broadband
  • Nordic Semiconductor (formerly Nordic VLSI), semiconductors
  • NRK, broadcasting (state-owned)
  • Norwegian, airline
  • Norwegian Computing Centre, software research
  • Norsk Hydro, oil and gas industry
  • Norske Skog, forestry
  • Norges Statsbaner, national train operator
  • Opera Software, software (mostly web browsers)
  • Opticom ASA, high-density computer memory research
  • Orkla, branded consumer goods, speciality materials and financial investments
  • Oslo Sporveier, municipality transit authority in Oslo
  • Pan Fish, fish farming
  • Petoro, oil and gas resources
  • Petrol Geo-Serv
  • Renewable Energy Corporation, solar energy
  • Scandinavian Airlines System, airline, joint Scandinavian
  • Schibsted, media
  • Sensonor, semiconductors (sensors)
  • Sinvest, Oil rig company
  • Snøhetta, architecture
  • Statoil, oil and gas
  • talk2me, customer relationship management
  • Tandberg, telecom (teleconferencing equipment)
  • Tandberg Data, computer storage equipment (streamers)
  • Tandberg Television, TV broadcasting equipment
  • Telenor, telecom
  • Telio, telecom
  • Tgs Nopec Geo
  • Think Nordic, electric vehicles
  • Tine, dairy products (Snøfrisk, Jarlsberg and Ski Queen cheeses)
  • Tomra Systems, soda/beer bottle/can return systems
  • Toromat1 Games, A small computer game company.
  • Trolltech, software (originator of Qt and related software)
  • TV 2, TV broadcaster
  • TVNORGE, TV broadcaster
  • Visma, software
  • Wallenius Wilhelmsen Lines, shipping
  • Widerøe, short-haul airline
[edit]

Historical

  • Framnæs shipyard, building of many ships, e.g. for whaling
  • Hafslund Nycomed, pharmaceutics
  • Indata, microcomputers
  • Magcom, cellular GSM phones
  • Mycron, microcomputers
  • Norsk Data, minicomputers
  • Tandbergs Radiofabrikk, radios, TVs, high end audio equipment
  • Tiki Data, microcomputers
