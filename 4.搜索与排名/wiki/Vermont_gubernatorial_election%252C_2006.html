Vermont gubernatorial election, 2006

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search
This article or section contains information about an upcoming or ongoing election.
Content may change dramatically as the election approaches and unfolds.

The 2006 Vermont gubernatorial election will take place on November 7, 2006. Incumbent Jim Douglas is expected to run for re-election. His recent approval rating was 62%. [1]

Candidates

[edit]

Democrats

  • Scudder Parker - Former Democratic Party Chair, State Senator, minister, and director of energy efficiency at the Vermont Department of Public Service. Parker began campaigning in late 2005. He won the Democratic nomination when no other Democrat filed by the July 16, 2006 deadline.[2] His fundraising efforts resulted in $110,000 in donations in the month of July 2006.
[edit]

Republicans

  • Jim Douglas - Incumbent Governor
[edit]

Other

  • Robert Skold - Liberty Union, Website Publisher. His running mate is Mary Alice Herbert.
