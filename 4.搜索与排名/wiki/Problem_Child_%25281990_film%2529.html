Problem Child (1990) is a film about an orphan child that deliberately wreaks havoc everywhere he goes. Nobody wants him until Ben Healy comes along. The script was thought to be a parody of the novel and 1956 film The Bad Seed.[1]

It starred the late John Ritter, his soon-to-be real-life wife Amy Yasbeck, Gilbert Gottfried, Jack Warden and child actor newcomer Michael Oliver as "Junior".

So credible a performance of evil was Oliver's first appearance on-screen, the demonic persona he portrayed became his signature role in that to this day it is still assumed by many that this was his real-life disposition.

Not a favorite with the critics, the film proved popular with the public and became a "surprise hit""[2] of the year, spawned two sequels, an animated series and remains popular to this day.

Plot

Spoiler warning: Plot and/or ending details follow.

An adoptive parent discovers that some children are given up by their biological parents for very good reasons in this dark comedy. Ben Healy (John Ritter) is a pleasant but brow-beaten yuppie working for his father Big Ben (Jack Warden), a tyrannical sporting goods dealer. Ben would love to have a son, but his wife Flo (Amy Yasbeck) has been unable to conceive. Ben approaches less-than-scrupulous adoption agent Igor Peabody (Gilbert Gottfried) with his dilemma, and Igor presents Ben and Flo with a cute seven-year-old boy, Junior (Michael Oliver). However, Junior is hardly a model child; mean-spirited and incorrigible, the child leaves a path of serious destruction in his wake, and is even pen pals with Martin Beck (Michael Richards), a notorious serial killer. After the cat ends up in the hospital, the house catches on fire, and Junior displays his effective but unethical method for winning in Little League, Ben is having serious doubts about Junior when Beck escapes from jail and decides to kidnap his faithful correspondent, along with Junior's new mom.

Box office

Problem Child proved to be a major box office success[citation needed], spawning two sequels and a TV series.

[edit]

DVD Release

Problem Child and Problem Child 2 were released on DVD in the U.S. on March 2, 2004, shortly after the death of John Ritter, as a package entitled Problem Child Tantrum Pack.

The package does not contain the third film, Problem Child 3: Junior in Love, which had a different cast.

The DVD was released as a single sided disc with both movies on a main menu. The DVD does not contain easter eggs[citation needed], although each of the submenus have their original theatrical trailer.

[edit]

Sequels

Due to the box-office success of Problem Child, there were two sequels: Problem Child 2 (1991) and the made-for-television Problem Child 3: Junior in Love (1995). The third film (in which Ritter, Oliver and Yasbeck do not appear) did very poorly[citation needed], and therefore the Problem Child franchise stopped.

Trivia

  • The poster for the movie was a spoof of the poster for Parenthood (1989).
  • It was considered by some to be offensive towards adopted children, particularly when Mrs. Healy yells (before deciding to adopt Junior), "I don't wear secondhand clothes, and I don't want a secondhand kid!"
  • The fake cat thrown at "Big Ben" causing him to fall down the stairs was obviously meant to offset any complaints of "animal cruelty".
  • In the scene where Ben Healy angrily walks up to Junior's bedroom is an obvious reference to the film The Shining (1980).
  • When Mr. Healy is about to spank Junior in his bedroom, the position of the globe on his bed changes.
  • When Ben is standing on the bridge watching the truck drive away with his wife in the back, he has the rifle in his hand. Ben and Junior turn around to walk back to the car, and the rifle disappears. It seems unlikely he would have left it laying on the bridge.
