Cleo Biography

Cleo is certainly not a stranger to the music industry having already worked with some of the best and well loved house hold names in the world she is back to put her own stamp on the workd with the release of her debut album (TBC)

Credited as a Singer, Songwriter, Actress, Spokeswomen for make-up giant Covergirl, and now mother Cleo has achieved so much already.

Since leaving Cleopatra Cleo has recorded two duets with tennor Russell Watson the first duet 'Someone Like You' on Russell's debut album 'The Voice' went muti platinum. Cleo join Russell on his europen leg of the tour and she join him to performed the song on the National Lottery when Cleo was 5 months pregnant with her first child, Chika Li Che. Cleo was replaced on the single release of the song by famed Steps star Faye Tozer. Cleo recorded her second duet with the tennor for his third album 'Reprise' named 'The Best That Love Can Be'.

In 2001 15,000 people packed the M.E.N Arena to watch Cleo and the young voices in concert break the record for the UK's largest choir which was 7000 choir members.

Cleo join a cast of star on ITV1's Songs Of Bond a tribute to the legdendary songs of James Bond covering For Your Eyes Only.

Cleo has toured with Joe Budden, Russell Watson, Blue, Girls Aloud and also embarked on a small Uni Tour.

On February the 3rd Cleo gave birth to her second child named Taylor James Mckenzie aka TJ.

Cleo's debut album is expected at the end of 06 and the title is yet to be confirmed

On her Official Myspace page she has put up an exclusive 'Album Sampler' for the fans.

The release of her brand new debut single 'Feelin' Like This' is expected the summer of 06.

Group Biography

Their debut single was "Cleopatra's Theme", which reached #3 in the UK charts in 1998. Their second single was "Life Ain't Easy", giving them their second Top 5 hit, going in at #4. They also released a cover of The Jacksons song "I Want You Back" which was a #4 hit.

Cleopatra were also part of the ensemble that released "Thank Abba For The Music".

They were signed to Madonna's US label Maverick Records in 1999.

They featured on the Bee Gees' tribute album, singing the album title track "Gotta Get A Message To You", and were asked to record a single for "Another Goofy Movie" by Disney. In 1999 they performed onboard the Disney cruise ship, with the concert being televisied in countries all over the world. Their U.S. debut single was "Cleopatra's Theme", which reached #26 on the Billboard Chart, and reached #16 on the R&B chart. They toured America with Jessica Simpson, Next and Brian McKnight and appeared on numerous TV shows, including on Jay Leno and The Queen Latifah Show.

They performed at the Nickelodeon Kids Choice Awards, being introduced by Madonna.

They were Covergirl spokeswomen, and sang and featured in the huge Covergirl campaign across America.

Cleopatra starred in their own #1 TV Sitcom "Cleopatra Comin Atcha!" and "In The House With". They also had a Christmas special.

They recorded their second album "Steppin Out" , which yielded the hit single "U Got It", in the US, working with Jam & Lewis, Dallas Austin, Daryll Simons and Stargate. They featured on Russell Watson's debut album 'The Voice', on the track 'Someone Like You'. They recorded a second duet 'The Best That Love Can Be' for his third album 'Reprise'.

Cleo has toured twice with Russell Watson On his national U.K & Ireland Tour, Blue, Girl's Aloudown. Cleo's new album is nearly complete and should be released soon.

In 2005 the band appeared on the ITV singing contest Hit Me Baby One More Time.
