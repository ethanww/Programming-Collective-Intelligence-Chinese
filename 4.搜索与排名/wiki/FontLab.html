FontLab

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

FontLab is both the name of a company, FontLab Ltd, and the former name of their flagship product, now called FontLab Studio. Since the early 2000's, FontLab Studio has been the dominant software tool for commercial/retail digital font development. This is partly because the once-dominant Fontographer by Altsys ceased development after its acquisition by Macromedia. During Macromedia's merger/acquisition by Adobe Systems in 2005, Macromedia sold Fontographer's rights and code to FontLab Ltd, so FontLab now owns and maintains both of the most popular font editing/development tools.

[edit]

History

Originally, there were two separate companies. SoftUnion Ltd of St. Petersburg, Russia made the software, under lead programmer Yuri Yarmola. Pyrus North America Ltd, of the USA, owned and operated by Ted Harrison, was formed in 1992 to distribute and market FontLab 2.0 for Windows, which came out in 1993 (there was no Mac version at the time). Eventually the two companies merged into a single entity, and today they have a wide range of font editing and conversion tools. Programming is still done by a Russian team in St. Petersburg, and management (Mr Harrison) moves between Canada and the USA.

FontLab's first Mac OS product was FontLab 3 for Mac, in 1998. Since then, FontLab (now FontLab Studio) has been issued for both Mac and Windows, but the Windows versions usually come out first, by six months to a year. Similarly, TransType comes out first in Mac OS versions, followed later by Windows versions.
