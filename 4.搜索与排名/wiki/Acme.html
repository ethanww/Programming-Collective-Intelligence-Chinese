Acme (disambiguation)

Acme (Greek ακμή, the peak, zenith, prime) denotes the best of something.

Look up acme in
Wiktionary, the free dictionary.

In contemporary culture and society it is used in diverse contexts:

  • Acme Corporation is a fictional company that exists in the Looney Tunes universe. Subsequently it has been adopted widely in all forms of fiction as the archetypal name for a fictional company or corporation (or generically a subject with juridical personality).
  • "The Acme Packers", a name used by the Green Bay Packers American football team in their first year in the NFL, when they were sponsored by the Acme Packing Company
  • ACME (album), is the sixth album by New York City blues/indie rock group the Jon Spencer Blues Explosion.
  • Acme (band), is a German metalcore band.
  • Acme (text editor), is a text editor and development environment originally for the Plan 9 from Bell Labs operating system.
  • Acme (ADL), an Architecture description language by Carnegie Mellon University
  • Acme, is the Perl software language's module namespace for bizarre or humorous software packages.
  • Acme thread form, is a screw thread used in specialized applications such as vises and leadscrews.
  • Acne, is a skin condition was originally called acme in Greek, but the spelling was corrupted.
  • USS Abarenda (IX-131), is a storage tanker previously known as the SS Acme.
  • Acme Mapper, is an online map who combines Google maps and Terraserver-USA.
