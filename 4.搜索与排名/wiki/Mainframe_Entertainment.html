Mainframe Entertainment

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Mainframe Entertainment is a Canadian computer animation and design company based in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada and Los Angeles, California, USA. It produces children's computer animation TV series.

On September 16, 2003, IDT Media announced that its animation unit, Digital Production Solutions, signed a binding Letter of Intent to acquire a controlling interest in Mainframe Entertainment, Inc. The acquisition was completed in December 2, 2003, making it part of IDT Entertainment. On May 16, 2006, IDT sold its IDT Entertainment division to Liberty Media. On July 20, 2006, it was announced that Rainmaker Income Fund would acquire IDT Entertainment's shares in Mainframe.[1]

Productions include:

  • ReBoot
  • Transformers Beast Wars
  • Shadow Raiders/War Planets
  • Weird-Oh's
  • Transformers Beast Machines
  • Spider-man
  • Action Man (not to be confused with the BKN series)
  • Heavy Gear
  • Max Steel (season 3 TV, Endangered Species, Forces of Nature)
  • Scary Godmother
  • Hot Wheels Highway 35 World Race
  • Hot Wheels AcceleRacers
  • Casper's Haunted Christmas
  • several Barbie fairy tale movies:
    • Barbie in The Nutcracker
    • Barbie as Rapunzel
    • Barbie of Swan Lake
    • Barbie as "The Princess and the Pauper"
    • Barbie and the Magic of Pegasus
  • Popeye's Voyage: The Quest for Pappy
  • the 1985 music video for "Money for Nothing", a song by Dire Straits (prior to Mainframe's founding proper) that won an MTV Video Music Award
  • Stuart Little 3: Call of the Wild
  • Inspector Gadget's Biggest Caper Ever: The Case Of The Giant Flying Lizard
  • Zixx series (Levels One and Two)
  • Beat Freaks
  • Arthur's Missing Pal
