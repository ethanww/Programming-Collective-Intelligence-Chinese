Patricia C. Dunn

Patricia C. Dunn, 53, serves as non-executive Chairman of the Board of Hewlett-Packard Company, a position she has held since February 2005, succeeding Carly Fiorina. She has been a Hewlett-Packard director since 1998. Ms. Dunn is also non-executive Vice Chairman of Barclays Global Investors since 2002.

Additionally, she is Director and Executive Committee member of Larkin Street Youth Services in San Francisco, on the board of the Conference Board's Global Corporate Governance Research Center, and an advisory board member of UC Berkeley Haas School of Business. [1]

[edit]

Career

Before her current Barclays Global Investors Vice Chairman role, Ms. Dunn worked there beginning in 1986 as a secretary and worked her way up to Chairman and CEO. She also worked as a freelance journalist.[1]

[edit]

Awards

Dunn received the Financial Women's Association of San Francisco "Financial Woman of the Year" award in 2001.[1]

[edit]

Education

Ms. Dunn graduated from UC Berkeley with degrees in economics and journalism.

[edit]

Controversy

Dunn is at the center of a controversy regarding her effort to ferret out board-level leaks to reporters in 2005-2006. HP hired companies that in the process of investigating the leaks, obtained the personal telephone records of HP board members and reporters who covered HP.[2]
