The 2006 North Indian cyclone season has no bounds, but cyclones tend to form between April and December, with peaks in May and November. These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical cyclones form in the northern Indian Ocean.

Season summary

[edit]

Tropical Storm 01A

On January 13, an area of convection organized into Tropical Storm 01A (also designated Tropical Storm 01B) to the southwest of the southern tip of India. Shortly after forming, conditions quickly became unfavorable, and the storm dissipated on January 14 over open waters.

[edit]

Very Severe Cyclonic Storm Mala (02B)

Main article: Cyclone Mala
Very Severe Cyclonic Storm Mala at peak intensity

An area of disturbed weather developed into Tropical Cyclone 02B on April 24 in the central Bay of Bengal. It later intensified into Cyclonic Storm Mala on April 25. Convection steadily increased, and under a favorable environment, the storm attained very severe cyclone strength, the equivalent to a Category 1 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale, on the 27th. It accelerated to the northeast, and intensified rapidly into an equivalent Category 4 cyclone on the Saffir-Simpson scale with wind speeds of over 185 km/h (115 mph) on the 28th, its peak intensity. It quickly weakened after peaking, made landfall on the 29th, and quickly dissipated over Myanmar.

Prior to making landfall, the cyclone lashed the western Myanmar coastline with powerful waves. Upon hitting the country, Mala caused severe structural damage. The area hardest hit was just outside of Yangon, with the strong winds damaging 150 buildings. The cyclone also downed numerous power lines, leaving many without electricity. [2] Mala dropped heavy rainfall throughout Myanmar, stopping drains in Yangon and causing flooding of up to 1 meter (3.28 feet) in depth. [3] The cyclone killed at least 22 people during its onslaught.

Cyclone Mala was the third intense cyclone to form in April; the other two were in 1991 and in 1994. Coincidentally, Cyclone Mala and the cyclones in 1991 and 1994 were all numbered as "02B", they are the only cyclones ever to form in April, and all of them made very destructive landfalls on the Asian mainland. [4]

[edit]

Tropical Storm 03B

Tropical Storm 03B

An area of tropical disturbance that formed near at the coastline of eastern India on around June 30, 2006. It slowly developed until it was classified as a tropical storm on July 2 by the Joint Typhoon Warning Center. The system crossed Orissa coast between Paradip and Chandbali at around 2030 IST on July 2. [5]
