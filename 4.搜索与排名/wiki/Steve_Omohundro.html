Steve Omohundro

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Stephen Omohundro is a scientist, university professor, author, software architect, and entrepreneur.

He graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Stanford University with Honors and Distinction in Physics and with Distinction in Mathematics. He received a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of California at Berkeley and published the book Geometric Perturbation Theory in Physics based on his thesis. [1]

The first company he worked for was Om Sonic Systems which designed and built custom music synthesizers. While working at the Thinking Machines Corporation, he co-developed Star Lisp with Cliff Lasser, the programming language for the Connection Machine. He then became a computer science professor at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where he co-founded the Center for Complex Systems Research. He wrote the three-dimensional graphics portion of Wolfram Research's Mathematica program as one of the original seven developers. [2]

In 1990, at the International Computer Science Institute (ICSI) in Berkeley, California, he led an international team in developing the object-oriented programming language Sather (recently featured in O'Reilly's poster of the History of Programming Languages). He also developed a variety of novel neural network techniques and machine learning algorithms and built systems which learned to read lips, control robots, and learn grammars. At the NEC Research Institute at Princeton University, he worked on a variety of applications of artificial intelligence and co-authored a patent on the PicHunter image database retrieval system. While at these institutions, he served on 6 conference program committees and 2 journal editorial boards, gave many invited talks, and produced 48 scientific publications. [3]

He founded Olo Software in Palo Alto to provide technology and business consulting to a variety of startup companies and research labs including InterTrust Technologies, Xerox PARC, Fuji-Xerox PAL, Ask Jeeves Inc., VideoScribe, LinuxMatix, Video Memoirs, and Molecular Objects. He founded Self-Aware Systems to develop a new kind of software that programs itself. [4]
