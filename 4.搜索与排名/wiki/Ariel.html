Ariel is a masculine name from the Hebrew אֲרִיאֵל "lion of God". The feminized versions are Ariella and Arielle. Other uses:

[edit]

People

  • Ariel Durant, American writer awarded with Pulitzer Prize.
  • Ariel Sharon, Prime Minister of Israel (2001-2006)
  • Ariel Ortega, El Burrito - The Little Donkey, an Argentinian footballer
  • Ariel Rot, Argentinian musician
  • Ariel Garten, Canadian artist
  • Ariel López Padilla, Mexican actor
  • Ariel Bercovich, Israeli poet
  • "Ariel" was a stage name for Aron Atwood in The Cuban Heels.
  • Ariel is an Puerto Rican Reggaeton singer.
  • Ariel is the current stage name for ECW star Shelly Martinez
  • Ariel Zilber, Israeli musician and songwriter
[edit]

Fictional characters

  • Ariel (Shakespeare), a spirit and servant of Prospero in William Shakespeare's play The Tempest
  • Princess Ariel, a red-haired mermaid who is fascinated by life on dry land in the 1989 Disney movie The Little Mermaid
  • Another Princess Ariel was a major character in the Thundarr the Barbarian animated TV series.
  • Ariel (Legacy of Kain), a spirit bound to the Pillars of Nosgoth in the Legacy of Kain video game series
  • Ariel is the chief of the sylphs serving Belinda in Alexander Pope's poem The Rape of the Lock.
  • Ariel is a former alias of the Marvel Comics character Kitty Pryde.
  • Ariel is a girl in New Jersey in the 1977 hit song "Ariel" by Dean Friedman.
  • Ariel is an Invid character in the science fiction series Robotech.
  • Ariel is the name of the unicorn traveling with Pete in Steven R. Boyett's book Ariel: Book of Change.
  • Ariel was the original name of the Transformers character, Elita One.
[edit]

Publications

  • Ariel (Beliaev), a science fiction novel by Russian author Alexander Beliaev
  • Ariel (Plath), the last book of poetry by Sylvia Plath
  • Ariel: The Book of Fantasy, a periodical published in the 1970s in book form
  • Ariel is the in-house magazine of the BBC, named after the statue of Shakespeare's Ariel at Broadcasting House.
  • Ariel is an essay on Latin-American culture and inter-American relations by José Enrique Rodo.
  • Ariel: a Shelley Romance is a novelized biography by André Maurois of Percy Bysshe Shelley, who drowned in the sailboat Ariel.
  • Ariel: Book of Change by Steven R. Boyett, tells the story of a young man and a unicorn who try to survive and find their place in a version of our world where technology ceases to function and magic returns.

Film

Television

Places

  • Ariel (city), an Israeli settlement in the West Bank between Nablus and Ramallah
  • Ariel (moon), a moon of Uranus discovered in 1851 by William Lassell and named for Pope's character above
  • Lake Ariel, Pennsylvania, a town and its namesake lake
  • Ariel (Firefly planet), a fictional planet in the science fiction TV series Firefly
  • Ariel, Washington, a town in southwestern Washington state
  • Ariel is one of the names of the city of Jerusalem, Israel

Commercial products

  • Ariel (washing powder), a washing powder brand of Procter & Gamble
  • Ariel (vehicle), a former manufacturer of motorcycles, bicycles and cars from 1871
  • Ariel Ltd, a modern British car company
  • Ariel Gallery, an artist's cooperative and gallery in Asheville, North Carolina
  • Ariel is a document delivery system from Infotrieve. It is often used by library interlibrary loan (ILL) departments. [1][2]
  • An operating system by Microware
  • A company that developed the ISPW in cooperation with IRCAM in France

Other

  • Five ships of the United States Navy have been named USS Ariel.
