The Hockey World Cup, sometimes called the Hockey World Championships, is an international field hockey competition organised by the International Hockey Federation (FIH). Started in 1971, the tournament is held every four years, bridging the four years between the Summer Olympics.

There is also a Women's Hockey World Cup, which has been held since 1974 and was organised by the International Federation of Women's Hockey Associations (IFWHA) until 1981.

Five countries have dominated the event in the ten tournaments held. Pakistan is the most successful team, having won the tournament four times; the Netherlands is second best with three titles. India, Australia and Germany won the tournament once each.

The Hockey World Cup 2006 currently held at the Warsteiner HockeyPark, Mönchengladbach, Germany from September 6 to September 17. Germany is also the current cup holder to date.

[edit]

History

The Hockey World Cup was first conceived by Pakistan's Air Marshal Nur Khan and India's Jimmy Nagarwalla. They proposed their idea to the FIH under the name of Patrick Rowley, the first editor of World Hockey magazine. Their idea was approved on October 26, 1969, and adopted by the FIH Council at a meeting in Brussels on April 12, 1970. It was decided that the inaugural World Cup would be held in October 1971.

The first tournament was to be hosted by Pakistan. However, political issues hindered the competition. Pakistan was surrounded by controversy when it invited India to compete in the tournament, only six years after the two countries had warred against each other. In 1971, there was also a civil war between West Pakistan and East Pakistan, now known as the Bangladesh Liberation War. This led to political instability throughout the country.

Pakistanis, led by cricketer Abdul Hafeez Kardar, protested against India's participation in the Hockey World Cup, threatening to burn down the stadium if the country's team was allowed to play. Meanwhile, they hijacked an Indian Airlines Fokker Friendship aircraft to Lahore Airport, where it was set on fire. Pakistan also threatened that, if India hosted the tournament, they would not participate.

With Pakistan not being able to host the tournament under the present political circumstances, and India not being allowed to by the Pakistanis, FIH decided in March 1971 to move the tournament to Real Club de Polo grounds in Barcelona, Spain, considered a neutral and peaceful European site. [1]

Although it is most common for twelve nations to participate in the tournament, no requirements or limitations on the size of the competition have been set by the FIH. The 1971 Cup included only ten nations, the fewest nations participating in the competition in World Cup history. This first tournament was won by Pakistan. The 1978 Cup featured fourteen nations. The 2002 Cup featured sixteen nations, the most nations to take part in World Cup history.

The first three tournaments were held regularly every two years. The 1978 Cup was the only tournament held three years from the previous tournament. Since 1982, the tournament has been held every four years, between the Summer Olympics field hockey competition.

[edit]

Trophy

The Hockey World Cup trophy was designed by the Bashir Moojid and created by the Pakistani Army. It was formally handed to FIH President Rene Frank by Mr H.E Masood, the Pakistani Ambassador to Belgium, on March 27, 1971, in Brussels. The trophy comprises a silver cup carrying an intricate floral design, surmounted by a globe of the world in silver and gold, all on a high blade base inlaid with ivory. On top of it is a model hockey stick and puck. The trophy stands 120.85 mm high without its base and 650 mm high with it. It weighs 11,560 g, including 895 g of gold, 6815 g of silver, 350 g of ivory and 3500 g of teak wood. [2]

[edit]

Format

In the World Hockey Cup, the qualification format is same as the final tournament format.

[edit]

Qualification

This stage has been a part of the Hockey World Cup since 1977. Commonly twelve teams feature in the qualification round, though in 1977 there were fourteen and in 2001 there were sixteen. The teams divide into two pools (four in 2001) and compete for a berth in the final tournament. The top two teams are automatically qualified and the rest of the berths are decided in playoffs.

[edit]

Final tournament

The final tournament features the continental champion and other qualified teams. Sometimes it also features the continental runners-up or the winners of the Summer Olympics' hockey competition. The teams divide into pools once more and play a round robin tournament. The top two teams in each pool will play in the semifinals for a place in the final. The bottom two teams in the semifinals will have a third place playoff. The rest of the teams will have playoffs to determine their final positions. If they are third or fourth in their pool, they will play for fifth place; if they are fifth or sixth in their pool, they will play for ninth place.

[edit]

Successful national teams

In all, 24 teams have already qualified for the Hockey World Cup (this includes the 2006 tournament). Of these, only eleven teams have made it to the semifinals and seven teams have made it through to the finals. The Soviet Union made it to the semifinals in fourth place in 1986, but their team no longer exists today.

With four titles, Pakistan is the most successful team in the Men's World Hockey Cup. The Netherlands follows with three titles. India, Australia and Germany tie for third place with one win apiece.

Pakistan is the only country that has won the tournament consecutively, in 1978 and 1982. The Netherlands is the only country that won as host, in 1973 and in 1998.

Pakistan and the Netherlands both reached the finals for three consecutive tournaments; the former in 1975, 1978, and 1982, and the latter in 1990, 1994, and 1998.

Germany made an unprecedented record after it reached the semifinals in nine consecutive tournaments, only missing the cut for the inaugural tournament in 1971. They are the current cup holders to date and the hosts of the 2006 Cup.

[edit]

Performance by host nations

Until 2002, there have been eight nations that have hosted the tournament. Only the Netherlands has won the tournament as host on both occasions in 1973 and 1998. Spain, England and Pakistan emerged runners-up respectively in the 1971, 1986 and 1990 tournaments. Australia is the only country that has emerged third place, which happened in the 1994 tournament in Sydney.

[edit]

Performance by continental zones

So far, the finals of the Hockey World Cup have been contested by three continental teams: Asian, European and Oceania. Asian has won five titles, followed by Europe with four titles and Australia is the only winner for Oceania. The Americas are the only continents that don't make appearances in any occasions of World Hockey Cup semi finals.
