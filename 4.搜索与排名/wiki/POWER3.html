POWER3

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search
Dual 375 MHz IBM POWER3-II processors on the CPU module of a RS/6000 44P 270.

In 1998, POWER3 was released. It was orginally was called the PowerPC 630 but was renamed, probably to differentiate the server oriented POWER processors it replaced from the more consumer oriented PowerPC. It implements the 64-bit PowerPC instruction set, including all of the optional instructions of the ISA (at the time), and had two floating-point units, three fixed-point units, and two load-store units.

POWER3 was used in IBM RS/6000 servers and workstations at 200 MHz. It had 15 million transistors on a 270 mm² die. Its successor, the POWER3-II was manufactured in a copper based process at .25 μm and reached 450 MHz on a 170 mm² die.

POWER3-II was succeeded by the POWER4.
