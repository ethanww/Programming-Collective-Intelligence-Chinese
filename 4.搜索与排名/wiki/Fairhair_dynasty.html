Fairhair dynasty

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

The Fairhair dynasty (Norwegian: Hårfagreætten) is traditionally regarded as the first royal dynasty of the united Kingdom of Norway. It was founded by King Harald I of Norway, known as Harald Fairhair or Finehair, around 890. King Håkon V Magnusson (r. 1299-1319) is regarded as being the last member of the dynasty. The descent back to Harald Hardrule (1046-1066) is undisputed, but it is a debated topic among modern historians whether he and his half-brother Saint Olav were in fact descended from Harald Fairhair and whether they in fact made such a claim, or whether this lineage is a construction from the 12th century. Sverre Sigurdsson's claim to be the son of Sigurd Munn is also usually considered to be false, which would make Inge Bårdsson the last king of the dynasty.
