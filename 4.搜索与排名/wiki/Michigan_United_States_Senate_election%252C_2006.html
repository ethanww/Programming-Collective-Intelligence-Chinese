Michigan United States Senate election, 2006

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search
This article or section contains information about an upcoming or ongoing election.
Content may change dramatically as the election approaches and unfolds.

The Michigan United States Senate election of 2006 will be held on November 7, 2006. Whoever is elected will serve between January 3, 2007, and January 3, 2013. Freshman Senator Debbie Stabenow is seeking a second term.

The filing deadline for major parties was May 16, 2006; the primary was on August 8, 2006.

  • Michael Bouchard - Oakland County sheriff; Bouchard's Campaign Website

Lost in primary

  • Keith Butler - reverend and former Detroit city councilman; Butler's Campaign Website

Failed to meet requirements

Jerry Zandstra - a minister and conservative activist; Zandstra's Campaign Website. He was campaigning for the Republican nomination, but failed to meet the petition requirements to get on the primary ballot.
