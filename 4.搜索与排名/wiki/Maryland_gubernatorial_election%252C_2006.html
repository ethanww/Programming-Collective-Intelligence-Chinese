The Maryland gubernatorial election of 2006 will be held on November 7, 2006, and will be a race for the Governor and Lieutenant Governor of Maryland. The winning candidates will serve from 2007 to 2011.

Background and candidates

The incumbent governor, Robert Ehrlich, was the first Republican elected to that position since Spiro Agnew in 1966. Ehrlich has the advantage of incumbency, but is expected to face a tough re-election battle in Maryland, a traditionally Democratic state. He was elected governor in 2002, and has also served in Congress and the Maryland House of Delegates. Because incumbent Lt. Governor Michael S. Steele is running for the U.S. Senate, Ehrlich chose Kristen Cox as his running mate on June 29, 2006. Cox serves as Maryland Secretary of Disabilities and has been a lobbyist for many years. She lives in Baltimore County, and is legally blind. [1]

The leading Democratic candidates for governor were initially Doug Duncan and Martin O'Malley. Duncan, who has been County Executive for Montgomery County, Maryland and was former mayor of Rockville, Maryland, dropped out of the race on June 22, 2006, having been diagnosed with clinical depression.[2] O'Malley, the current Mayor of Baltimore, remains in the race and has been outpolling Ehrlich as of July 2006 (see below). O'Malley selected Anthony G. Brown, who is a Delegate from Prince George's County and Iraq War veteran, as his running mate. [3].

As of the beginning of 2006, Ehrlich had over $8 million in campaign cash while O'Malley has $4 million .

The Green Party has fielded candidate Ed Boyd, who is a recruiter for a temporary employment agency.[4] James Madigan, a Lieutenant in the Maryland Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services, was chosen as the running mate for Ed Boyd.[5]

The Populist Party of Maryland has fielded a ticket of Christopher Driscoll for Governor and Ed Rothstein for Lt. Governor.
